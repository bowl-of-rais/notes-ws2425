>[!info] $A^{-1}$ for which $AA^{-1} = A^{-1}A = I$

iff $A$ is invertible:
- $A^{T}$ is invertible
- $A$ has full rank, aka $\det A \neq 0$
- $0$ is not an eigenvalue of $A$

### Properties

- $(A^{-1})^{-1} = A$
- $(kA)^{-1} = k^{-1}A^{-1}$
- $(A^{T})^{-1} = (A^{-1})^{T}$
- $\det A^{-1} = (\det A)^{-1}$


-> compute via [Gaussian elimination](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gaussian_elimination)
